## Overview

This dashboard presents a comprehensive analysis of the World Happiness Report dataset, covering multiple years and highlighting global and regional performance. It explores socio-economic and lifestyle variables influencing happiness levels across countries.

## Objective

To explore global and regional happiness scores and the factors affecting them.

## Key Metrics

- Happiness Score
- GDP per Capita
- Social Support
- Life Expectancy
- Freedom
- Generosity
- Corruption Perception

## Insights and Findings

- Western Europe ranks highest region, with Switzerland, Iceland, and Denmark among the top performers.
- GDP per Capita and Social Support strongly correlate with Happiness Score, suggesting that economic stability and strong social networks heavily influence well-being.
- Countries with high corruption perception scores tend to have lower happiness rankings, indicating the importance of trust in institutions.
- Several developing regions display lower happiness score primarily due to reduced economic opportunities and lower social support levels.
- Yearly trend analysis shows only gradual changes, suggesting that national happiness is stable but influenced by global economic and political changes.

## Recommendations

- Improve social support systems and welfare packages
- Enhance transparency and anti-corruption measures
- Promote healthcare access, freedom, safety, and social inclusion.

## Conclusion

World Happiness is driven by economic strength, social cohesion, and trust in institutions. Sustainable development policies that prioritize social welfare, transparency, and health can significantly improve national happiness
